Courses Available

Here at Arm's Reach, we believe the best way to empower young people is to give them the skills to empower themselves. We strive to give high quality classes in basic life skills that students may not know themselves or need a better teacher, or guidance to develop further. We work with our students and mentors so that each participants is set up for success.

Designed with beginning chef's and baker's in mind, Cooking 101 is a group class designed to teach cooking and life skills from the ground up- both in basic cooking techinques, sourcing healthy food, and deciding whats best for the money. This class focuses on seasonal recipes to use whats on hand- graduates can retake this class for an added challenge, help new students and learn more skills.
Pre-req's: None

Taught By: John

Duration: 6 Weeks

Time: Mondays, 17:30-19:30

This class takes the basics from Cooking 101 and expands on them, encouraging students to develop their own weekly meal plan and prep, with the guidance of experienced chef's. Students will bring their own meals to share each week, recieve feedback and ideas for future weeks. This course will have a heavy focus on seasonal recipes, and can be repeated.
Pre-req's: Cooking 101

Taught By: John

Budgeting and saving money seem like an easy thing in theory, but in reality, can be very challenging to in practice with no guidance. Led by program leader, Mary, this course teaches the principles of saving by choosing short term goals for students to work towards and applies those skills for long term goals as determined by the students.
Pre-req's: None

Taught By: Mary

Duration: 4 Weeks

Time: Tuesdays 16:30-17:30

This short course is designed to teach students better ways to structure their days and manage their tasks,whether geared for school, higher education or professional work- anyone can benefit.

Sewing is a skill that can be both a hobby as well as a household nessicity. Students will be taught both pratical skills- such as hemming and sewing buttons, as well as how to read patterns to make basic projects such as a purse, scarf or a hat. This course can be retaken, students will work on more complex projects if they do. Materials provided the first time, and available for a discounted price if re-taking.
Pre-req's: None

Taught By: Laura

Duration: 6 Weeks

Time: Thursdays, 17:30-20:30

Knowing how to fix minor household problems such as a leaky faucet or clogged toilet can be a time saver, as well as a money saver- no need to call anyone if you can repair it yourself! Even if you don't plan on owning your own home, having the knowledge can be an immense help. This one day course focuses on student directed questions and problems.
Pre-req's: None
